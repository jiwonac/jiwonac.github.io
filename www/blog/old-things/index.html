Old Things and Simple Living

Some time ago, I dropped my Macbook Pro into water. The water damage was unrepairable. I am a student on a tight budget, so I bought a $200 refurbished Thinkpad off of Amazon which was what I could afford.

I installed a minimal Linux distro, and threw on the lightweight XFCE desktop environment. It does everything I need to do. If you're a creative that needs Adobe software or graphics horsepower, then your mileage may vary. For my needs though, this five-year-old machine runs snappily. I can watch videos, browse the web, write documents, and do some programming. I can even run demanding software by SSHing into a server that does the heavy lifting.

What I didn't expect is how sometimes it is nice to use old things.
