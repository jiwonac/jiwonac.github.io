On Analog Tactility

As an older zoomer, I was still around for casette tapes, printing stacks of papers in binders, and paper dictionaries. So it's hard not to get excited about new gadgets and apps. At the end of the day, I'm a computer scientist and a technology enthusiast.

While my life is much more digital than it was when I was younger, not all analog-to-digital transition stuck. Why can't I imagine going back on some domains, but have gone back on others? The following are a couple of reasons I could think of.

When Does Analog Shine?

Physicality

By physicality, I mean the presence that analog objects occupy in the real world. If it physically exists, then it's easier to keep it in my mental space, to remember that it exists. This is particularly important for obligations. For instance, I have never been able to stay productive by using digital calendars and todo lists. I always forget their existence, and struggle with tardiness. I need a small notepad that stays in my pocket at all times.
